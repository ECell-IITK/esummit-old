E- Cell IIT K presents “Stock the Stock”, now show off your Fin Gyan by trading in a live stock trading competition. Apply your aptitude, analyse the market fluctuations, make a profitable bidding and let market play its game. Get ready for a power packed environment and experience unlimited fun. Participating teams will need to fill up this form. Online submission of the form must be done by 20th August. Shortlisting for the next round will be done on first come first serve basis.

Structure

Two Step Registration:
All participating startups have to register via two step process.The participants need to fill the concerned details and top startups will be shortlisted for the Final Round Mentoring sessions, to be held in IIT Kanpur during eSummit'17.

Intensive Mentoring Sessions:
Ten shortlisted startups will have a Four - Week mentoring session with our esteemed mentors where they will get to discuss their ideas and business plans to build a proper legal, operational and structural framework. This will be followed by intensive face to face mentoring and brainstorming sessions with a VC from the panel of Upstart. Along with invaluable advice from one of the most experienced Entrepreneur-cum-Mentors in the country, startups also get to enhance their network manifold with help from them.

Elevator Pitch:
The teams have to pitch their summary to an independent panel in under 200 seconds. It is a prerequisite to the finale and will be held in campus on 2nd September. It has a 20% weightage in the total evaluation. The best pitches will also receive a cash prize.

Final Pitch:
After the session, Startups will have time till the next day to build upon their Plan and modify their Pitch based on the inputs they received. They will then participate in a 10-minute pitch (followed by Q&As) to present their improved Business Plans before the Upstart panel, a panel of 5 judges on 3rd September. The start-ups will accordingly be given their final positions based on the Judging Criteria as elaborated in a later section.

Judging Criteria:

  • Concept
    • Originality of Idea.
    • Scalability, in terms of the potential for growth and replication of the venture.
    • Well-conceived implementation plan and timetable.
    • Overall likelihood of achieving demonstrable results.
  • Market
    • Evidence of demand for the venture in the marketplace; market size and growth opportunity.
    • Demonstrated knowledge of target market
    • Competitive analysis , SWOT analysis
    • Marketing Plan addresses (wherever applicable): positioning, pricing, advertising, promotion, packaging, timetable.
  • Performance Benchmarks
    Outline the development of systems to track, monitor and assess the performance milestones articulated in venture plan
  • Financial Aspects
    • Fundability: The potential for the venture to receive financial support from additional investors/donors(including opportunities for funding from foundations, social venture funds, angel investors and venture capitalists)
    • The rigor and feasibility of the venture's financial model and goals
    • Potential for follow-on funding to grow the venture through self-sufficiency or profitability.
  • Risk Assessment and Contingency Plans
    Identify risk factors associated with the enterprise and external environment. Develop a plan for mitigating them.
  • Demonstrated Leadership Ability
    Quantify venture leader's ability to develop people, promote change and achieve results
  • Improvement
    Improvement in the business plan from original proposal, post the mentoring session, will be taken into consideration.
